i. What is a Refr Campaign?

• A Refr campaign provides all the details of the reward structure you give out to your customers for sharing your store & making a successful recommendation.

• The rewards can be a flat cashback or a percentage.

• Each campaign has a minimum order value a customer has to fulfil to be eligible for a reward.

However, Refr gives complete freedom to all your customers to recommend your store post a purchase even if they don’t fulfil the minimum order value required, they will just not receive the cashback.

ii. How many campaigns can you make?

You can make as many campaigns as you wish, according to your store’s requirements. Different campaigns can have differences in the cashback type-flat amount or percent, with different time durations and varying minimum order values to suit all customers.

iii. What is the minimum duration for one campaign?

The minimum duration for each campaign is 1 month.

iv. How are the rewards paid out to your customers?

Refr gives you a guaranteed return on your marketing spends unlike other marketing avenues. You pay only when you get a sale.

How does this work?

A customer makes a purchase at your store, loves the experience & thus recommends your store to friends, family & followers via personalised recommendations. Today, you reward this customer with a small cashback for being your brand advocate.

Friends & followers of this customer come back using the recommendation, giving you a fresh sale. For this, you reward the new customer with a cashback of a higher amount & at the same time reward your existing customer with a small cashback for making a sale conversion for you.

You give out all these rewards in the form of Refr cash, which gets debited from your campaign wallet balance. [See section 5(ii) for campaign wallet]

v. Tips on making a campaign

- Set your minimum order value in a way that best suits your business allowing maximum customers to meet the requirement.

- Keep your rewards smaller for point of sale purchases & higher for purchases made by friends which give you new sales.

-In this way you will make a campaign that attracts new customers while also appreciating your existing customers to recommend your store.

- Campaigns once made cannot be edited, so make sure you make them wisely.

- You can always create a new campaign from the campaign section, in case you want to set different variables of rewards.

i. Store Wallet –

This wallet stores the money obtained when anyone pays you with Refr Cash and through online payment.

Funds from this wallet can be transferred to the campaign wallet.

Payments can be accepted via e-wallets, net banking, credit & debit cards.

Funds can be withdrawn at any time from this wallet. 5% will be charged for withdrawal of funds.

A ledger is maintained for all transactions.

ii. Campaign Wallet –

This wallet keeps your Refr campaigns running. Your campaign rewards are given out via this wallet.

You can add funds to this wallet selecting from the different tier options or can transfer funds from your store wallet.

Every time you refill your Campaign wallet, a processing fee is charged, which varies as per the different tiers.

Funds can be withdrawn from this wallet in case they are unused for a period of one year.

Redeem request is the request an offline & hybrid store receives when a customer is at the checkout of a physical store. Once the merchant approves this request & accepts the payment, only then the cashback is credited to the customer. Redeem requests are shown on the dash.

Once a request is accepted, it can be seen in the “Recent Orders” section on the dash.

The icon on the top right side of each request depicts how the request has been made. A two figure icon indicates a recommendation from a friend, where the reward has to be given to both the user & the friend. A single icon indicates a user’s discovery journey where the reward is given only to that user.

Recent order on the other hand is an online order which an online & hybrid store receive via the Refr platform. It has all details including the order items, order value, time & date & the cashback given.
